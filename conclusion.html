Attorney-Client Privilege

Understanding the rules of legal confidentiality — with help from TV shows

What is Attorney-Client Privilege?

Attorney-client privilege is a legal rule that protects private conversations between a lawyer and their client. If a client is seeking legal advice, the lawyer can’t be forced to reveal what was said — even in court. The goal is to help clients speak openly without fear of exposure.

Important: This privilege only applies when legal advice is being sought. It does not apply if the conversation is meant to help commit a future crime — that’s called the crime-fraud exception.

Attorney-Client Privilege in Pop Culture / TV

Breaking Bad – Saul Goodman & Walter White

Saul Goodman is Walter White’s “criminal lawyer." When Walter talks to Saul about legal risks, that’s privileged. But when they plan how to hide / launder money or intimidate witnesses? That’s not protected because it involves crimes, but in the show Saul Goodman tries to practice Attorney Client Privilege as he is working for Walter to perform these illegal tasks.

Suits – Harvey Specter & His Clients

Harvey often reassures clients that their conversations are confidential. That’s true — if it’s legal advice. But if someone brings a friend into the room or talks about doing something illegal, those protections can vanish.
